**Survey of Budgeting Tools**

1.YNAB (You Need A Budget)
YNAB is a well-known personal budgeting application using a zero-based budgeting system, which means every dollar of income is assigned to a purpose of use. YNAB supports bank syncing, detailed spending reports, and goal tracking. This increases convenience and helps users stay on track. However, it requires a paid subscription for $14.99/month or $109/year. This application also assumes users are willing to manage their finances actively and frequently, which may not be suitable for a casual user.

2.Splitwise
Splitwise is designed for tracking expenses among groups. It simplifies bill splitting and makes it effective for collaborative spending. However, it lacks various features like categorization and deep financial analysis. It is a perfect tool for splitting rent with roommates or settling a dinner tab, but it is not suitable as a personal budgeting tool.

3.Mint
Mint is a popular budgeting app that automatically syncs with bank accounts to track transactions. It’s incredibly convenient because it builds visual charts for you according to your data, and offers alerts for unusual spending. Although it’s much more convenient compared to other tools, it relies on automatic data entry, which can lead to incorrect categorization and raises privacy concerns due to data sharing.

4.PocketGuard
PocketGuard keeps it simple by showing the money left in the user's pocket. It is very straightforward and reduces workload for users who do not demand for complex budgeting systems. While a limited free version is available, the full premium subscription is priced at $12.99 per month or $74.99 per year. The free version offers limited customization and less detailed analysis compared to other financial tools. Although PocketGuard uses strong encryption to ensure data security, its reliance on cloud storage and bank syncing puts it on the lower end of the privacy scale. 

5.Goodbudget
Goodbudget allocates funds into virtual categories called envelopes, users can assign transactions to the corresponding envelope. Goodbudget requires manual data entry and does not support automatic bank syncing with its free plan, making it less convenient for users. However, this tool does not store bank passwords or actual transaction data, it uses a unique identifier called a "FITID" to track transactions, so it’s more secure than other automated apps.

6.Google Sheets / Microsoft Excel
Google Sheets or Excel are the most flexible tools, users can design their own budgeting systems. They have full control over categories, formulas, and analysis. On the other hand, this method requires a good technical knowledge to set up, and much more effort on the manual data entry.
